Epipelagic Zone

Around 90% of all biomass in the ocean is located close to the surface in the first 200 meters. Enough sunlight enters these waters to allow photosynthesis. In the open waters, photosynthesis is peformed by phytoplankton: microscopic floating algae. These are eaten by zoo plankton - small animals which drift in the ocean currents. In turn, the zoo planktons are eaten by a huge variety of other animals. These range from small fish like sardines to giant manta rays, whale sharks and even the blue whales.
